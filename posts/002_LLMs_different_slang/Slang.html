Introduction

I am interested in interpreting how LLMs (GPT-40) interpet slang. In other words, when prompted speak like a “girl” or speak like a “boy” I wonder how the two would be different. I also wonder how LLMs will interpret me saying speak like a “New Yorker” vs speak like a “Bostonian.” I will prompt GPT-40 to describe to me how to make pizza using slang from these different “types” of people.

Prompts:

  1. Describe to me how to make a Pizza like a girl would
  2. Describe to me how to make a Pizza like a boy would
  3. Describe to me how to make a Pizza like a New Yorker would
  4. Describe to me how to make a Pizza like a Bostonian would

I also prompted GPT-40 to be succint in their answers. Sometimes it would not spit out the direct response, so I prompted again “talk like a girl would.”

Prompt 1: Describe to me how to make a Pizza like a girl would

Answer: Hey there! ❤️ Ready to make the cutest pizza ever? Let’s go! What You Need: Dough: Homemade or store-bought—whatever makes life easier. Sauce: Something tomato-y and delicious. Cheese: Mozzarella is a must, but a cheese party never hurt anyone. Toppings: Go wild—pepperoni, veggies, pineapple, you name it! Let’s Do This: Roll It Out: Dust some flour, and roll that dough! Try a heart shape for some extra cuteness. Sauce Time: Spread that yummy sauce on, leaving a little border for the crust. Think of it as painting—get creative! Say Cheese: Load on the cheese. Maybe make fun shapes or spell something out! Top It Off: Time to party with the toppings. Make a smiley face or a cute pattern. Go artsy! Bake with Love: Pop it in the oven at 475°F (245°C) for about 10-15 mins until it’s golden and bubbly. Finishing Touch: Add some fresh herbs or a sprinkle of red pepper flakes for a little zing! Don’t Forget: Chill Vibes: Crank up your fave tunes. Dance around a bit; it’s a pizza party! Share the Love: Invite some friends or fam to join in. The more, the merrier, right? Enjoy every cheesy, delicious bite! 🍕💕

Analysis: GPT-40 used a lot of hearts and exclamation marks, perhaps indicating that women are more likely to show more enthusiasm/emotional. There is a bit of a “ditzy” with words such as “extra cuteness,” “yummy,” and “artsy.” I think GPT-40 is adhering to typical gender stereotypes, but it seems like this LLM’s understanding of a classic “girly” response also meant it lose its seriousness.

Prompt 2: Describe to me how to make a Pizza like a boy would

Response: Hey man, ready to make some epic pizza? Let’s get to it! What You Need: Dough: Store-bought or homemade, your call. Sauce: Your favorite tomato pizza sauce. Cheese: Mozzarella works great, but add what you like. Toppings: Whatever you’re into—pepperoni, peppers, mushrooms, you name it. Steps: Roll It Out: On a floured surface, roll out your dough to your preferred thickness. Sauce It Up: Spread that sauce evenly, leaving room for the crust. Cheese Time: Cover with mozzarella. Feel free to add a mix for extra flavor. Top It: Add your chosen toppings. It’s your pizza, load it up how you like. Bake: Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C). Cook for 10-15 minutes until the crust is golden and cheese is bubbling. Finish: Add some fresh herbs if you’re feeling fancy. Extras: Tunes: Put on some music. Keeps the vibes good. Chill and Enjoy: Invite a buddy or grab a cold drink. Good times ahead! Dig in and enjoy—you’ve earned it! 🍕

Analysis: I find this one interesting because it is more succint, abrupt, and to the point. There’s also a mention of alcohol, which I find interesting as well. I think, overall, the “boy” version is much more serious than the girl version—maybe playing into stereotypes of men being more formal/straight to the point than women.

Step 3: Describe to me how to make a Pizza like a New Yorker would

Response: Alright, listen up! We’re making a classic New York slice, so let’s do this right. What You Need: Dough: Go for that high-gluten stuff, gives you the right chew. Grab it from a local joint if you ain’t making it. Sauce: Keep it simple. Crushed tomatoes, a drizzle of olive oil, some garlic, pinch of oregano. Cheese: Low-moisture mozzarella, none of that fancy stuff. Toppings: Keep it classic. Maybe some pepperoni, or just cheese if you’re a purist. Here’s the Deal: Roll the Dough: Get it nice and thin, alright? You want a big, floppy slice. Sauce It Up: Light layer of sauce, not too much! We ain’t making soup here. Cheese Layer: Cover with shredded mozzarella. Go generous—don’t be stingy. Toppings: Throw on some pepperoni if you’re feeling it. Bake: Crank your oven as high as it goes—500-550°F, got it? Use a pizza stone if you got one. Bake till that crust is golden and the cheese has those little brown spots. Slice It: Big slices, none of those tiny cuts. Gotta be foldable, ya know? Final Touch: Extras: Hit it with some Parmesan and red pepper flakes if you like a kick. Eat Up: Grab a soda or a cold beer, and enjoy it hot outta the oven. Now, dig in and enjoy! Just like we do it in New York. 🍕

Analysis: With family from New York, I found this one really fun! I think it definitely adhered to the stereotypes of New Yorkers being loud and energetic. Using slang like ‘ain’t,’ ‘ya know?’ and ‘outta’ also maybe adheres to a type of New Yorker they are trying to get at. I also noticed it’s more succint as well compared to the “girl.”

Prompt 4: Describe to me how to make a Pizza like a Bostonian would

Response: Hey, kid, let’s whip up a wicked good pizza, Boston-style. Here’s how we’re gonna do it. What You Need: Dough: Make it a bit thicker, give it that hearty New England feel. Sauce: Rich tomato sauce with some garlic and herbs tossed in. Cheese: Load it up with mozzarella and maybe toss in some cheddar for good measure. Toppings: Whatever you’re in the mood for. Sausage, peppers, maybe some of that Boston baked bean vibe if you’re adventurous. The Process: Roll Out the Dough: You want a medium-thick crust, something that holds up—a real chowda of a crust. Ladle on the Sauce: Be generous with the sauce, we ain’t skimpin’ here. Cheese It Up: Cover it with mozzarella and throw some cheddar if you’re feeling it. We like it cheesy! Top It Off: Add your toppings. Be creative or keep it classic—dealer’s choice, kid. Bake It: Set the oven to around 425°F. Let it cook till the crust is golden and the cheese bubbles. About 15-20 minutes oughta do it. Slice It Up: Cut it into nice, hearty slices. Final Touches: Finish Strong: Maybe sprinkle some fresh herbs or a dash of olive oil if you’re putting on airs. Savor It: Pair it with a cold Sam Adams or your favorite local brew. Now go on, chow down and enjoy your pizza, Boston-style!

Analysis: This response was similar to the New York Style Response. It was very succint and to the point, while also having a little touch of slang such as “wicked” “kid” and “local brew.” I also noticed there was specificity within the alcohol touch such as “Sam Adams.” I also think it is interesting how both the New York and Boston “tone” align more closely with GPT-40s interpretation of a “male” slang rather than female slang.

Conclusion:

All in all, I think GPT-40s adhered to stereotypes on City and Gender. However, I do think Boston’s was less of a “Bostonian” culure and more just that of a man. I noted before that GPT-40 used similarly language with New York and Boston as it did with “male.” I wonder if it has to do with the actual food–pizza. Maybe if I asked GPT-40 to make a salad for example, it would have had more of a stereotypically feminine touch.
